DECISION UNDER REVIEW
On 21 April 1998 the Department notified the Visa Applicant care of her legal
advisers that her application for a Business (Long
Stay) visa Subclass 457
was refused.
The decision maker noted in part that the Visa Applicant failed to satisfy
clauses 457.111(b) and 457.223(7) of the
Migration Regulations 1994
The Visa Applicant applied to MIRO on 25 May 1998 for a review of the decision
to refuse her application for a Subclass 457 visa.
On 13 August 1998 the primary decision to refuse the Visa Applicant's
application for a Subclass 457 visa was affirmed by MIRO.
The MIRO review officer noted in part:
"I find that the applicant is unable to satisfy the criteria of parts (2), (3),
(4), (5), (6), (7), (8) or (9) of 457.223.
As essential criteria have not been met by the applicant, no assessment against
the remaining criteria is required, the application
for a visa subclass 457 -
Business (Long Stay) cannot be granted.
I note the applicant appears to have undertaken business activity in Australia
whilst the holder of a visa subject to condition 8101
"no work", a letter from
Miliconcept Catering Pty Ltd is signed by her a Director on 15 October 1996.
Subsequent to the grant of
a bridging visa A with a 8107 condition "cannot
change employer or occupation", which at that time was non working visitor or
best Director of Miliconcept the applicant actively worked at the China
Gourmet Restaurant, I refer to statements made at interview
and a letter from
her agent dated 13 January 1998. These activities would appear to have been in
breach of visa conditions and would
need to be considered in any assessment
against regulation 457.221. I have not made any assessment against this
regulation as I
have already determined the applicant fails to meet an
essential criteria."
The Visa Applicant was notified of the decision direct by registered mail on 13
August 1998.

As indicated above, in order to succeed in an application for a visa under the
Migration Regulations
, it is necessary for the Visa Applicant to satisfy all of
the criteria for the grant of the visa.
At the time of the application in October 1996 the Tribunal notes that the Visa
Applicant was the holder of a Class TN Subclass 686
visitor visa. This class
of visa is listed as one of the specified classes of visa in clause 457.211 of
the Regulations. Therefore
the Visa Applicant satisfies that criteria.
Given the evidence and material before it the Tribunal finds that the Visa
Applicant is unable to satisfy subclauses 457.223(2),
(3), (5), (6), (8) and
(9) of the Regulations.
The Tribunal notes that the Visa Applicant was nominated by Miliconcept for a
"key activity"
with that Australian company that was registered the day
before her application for the Subclass 457 visa. Miliconcept has withdrawn
its nomination and sponsorship of the Visa Applicant as noted in the letter to
the Department in April 1998. As well the Tribunal
notes that there is no
approved business nomination by the Department for Miliconcept. Consequently
the Visa Applicant is unable
to satisfy sub clause 457.223(4) of the
Regulations.
