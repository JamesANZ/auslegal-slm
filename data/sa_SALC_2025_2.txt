plainly is a matter that a licensing authority is obliged to take into account

is not to say that because a new licence will cause some interference to

address this. The Act
promotes the granting of new licenses in recognition of their importance to the

industries, such as tourism. It becomes a

some interference to others. Ultimately this calls for a judgment as

be refused outright, or modified.
this case, the extent of the interference did not appear to be
self‑evidently unreasonable. In those circumstances, it seemed
an appropriate way to deal with the matter might be to allow the application on
an interim basis to ascertain how the
venue with its new licence would operate.
