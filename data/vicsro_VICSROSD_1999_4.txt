The ruling updates SD 063 to reflect increases in the
thresholds applicable to first home buyers who
qualify for exemptions under sections 71A and 71B
Stamps Act 1958
This ruling sets out the eligibility and application

First Home Buyers
Section 71B of the Act provides full stamp duty relief
to first home buyers where the value of the house
and land being purchased does not exceed
$115,000, and partial relief where the value of the
house and land is greater than $115,000 but does
not exceed $165,000. Where the value of the house
and land exceeds $165,000 no relief is available. To
qualify, applicants must have purchased the property
for full market value, although a benefit is also
available where the purchase involves a commercial
discount. No benefit is available where the
acquisition of property from the transferor is by way
All purchasers of the property must be included in
the application for relief and must be natural
persons (ie. either a single person, or a person and
the person’s spouse or de facto spouse).
The applicant must have either one or more
dependent children on the date the contract is
entered into, or one or more dependant children
within 11 months after the date the contract was
entered into. (A dependent child is a child under the
age of 18 years who ordinarily resides with and is the
responsibility of the purchaser),
Where the purchaser has one dependent child, the
combined taxable income of the applicant(s) must
not exceed $39,000 in the relevant financial year, or
$40,000 if the purchaser has two or more
dependent children.
Each applicant must reside, or intend to reside in the
property as their principal place of residence and
must not have previously owned, either individually
or jointly, real property anywhere in Australia which
was used as their principal place of residence.

Section 71A of the Act provides full stamp duty relief
to home buyers with concession cards where the
value of the house and land being purchased does
not exceed $100,000, and partial relief where the
value of the house and land is greater than
$100,000 but does not exceed $130,000. No relief
is available where the value of the house and land
exceeds $130,000. To qualify, applicants must have
purchased the property for full market value
although a benefit is also available where the
purchase involves a commercial discount. No benefit
is available where the acquisition of property from
the transferor is by way of a gift.

Application requirements
To obtain the benefit applicants must complete the
‘Benefits for first home buyers with families’ or
‘Benefits for home buyers with concession cards’
application forms, available from the State Revenue
Office. Applicants’ attention is drawn to the statutory
declaration contained in the form and the reference
to penalties that apply where false or misleading
information results in the granting of a benefit.
An applicant who is eligible for a stamp duty benefit
on a transfer of land may also apply for a stamp duty
benefit on a mortgage over that property.
The mortgage benefit available is the stamp duty
payable on a mortgage up to the amount required to
complete the contract.
