Contributions to the Construction Industry Long Service Leave Fund
Revenue Rulings
Construction Industry Long Service Leave
1997, employers in the building and construction
industry are required to contribute to a portable long
service leave scheme. This scheme provides long
service leave benefits to employees employed in the
industry. The portable long service leave scheme in
Victoria is administered by CoINVEST Ltd as trustee
of the Construction Industry Long Service Leave Fund
(the Fund).
Since 1 July 1993, the definition of wages under the
Pay-roll Tax Act
(the Act) includes fringe benefits
as defined in the
Fringe Benefits Tax Assessment Act
1986 (Cth) (the FBT Act) and the taxable value of
those benefits will be calculated in accordance with the
The purpose of this Revenue Ruling is to clarify
whether Pay-roll Tax is payable on contributions made
to the Fund.
Contributions to the Fund are not subject to Pay-roll
Tax as long as they are not taxable under the FBT Act.
To determine whether or not contributions to the Fund
are taxable under the FBT Act, please contact the
Australian Taxation Office.
Payments of long service leave benefits made out of
the Fund are not taxable because the Commissioner
of State Revenue does not regard these payments as
wages for Pay-roll Tax purposes.
