consultations
with the three patients.
The panel conducted an Inquiry on 23 September 2005.
On the basis of the evidence before it, the panel decided that Dr
IJ had contravened
the required standard of practice, but that Dr IJï¿½s
conduct did not put public safety at risk. Accordingly, the Panel
decided that
it was appropriate to formally reprimand Dr IJ.
