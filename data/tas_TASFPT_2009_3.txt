D Graham v Forest Practices Authority [2009] TASFPT 3 (29 April 2009)
File No:02/08F D3/2009
D Graham Appellant
Forest Practices Authority Respondent
This was the hearing of an appeal against the refusal of Forest Practices
Plan BRM0421 at 1016 Millwood Road King Island
The appeal was heard
at Hobart on 16 April 2009
Mr Graham appeared in
Graham Wilkinson appeared on behalf of the Forest Practices
Tribunal finds and decides that clearing of Mr Graham’s land on
King Island will not result in native vegetation remaining
at or below 30%
of the Island’s area of original cover and thereby meets the requirements
on the “Tasmanian Government
Policy for Maintaining a Permanent Native
Forest Estate.”
It will be necessary for the Appellant to demonstrate that the clearing of
the land will not adversely affect threatened species or
threatened native
vegetation communities, for which a survey will be necessary.
followed discussion as to the time and time of year best suited for such survey.
It was determined that the best time for a flora
and fauna survey to identify
threatened species on the affected land would be spring.
appeal is further adjourned to Thursday 10 September 2009
Dated this 29
day of April 2009
Mr J Pretty
Mr P Wright
Mr R Brereton
Deputy Chief Chairman
