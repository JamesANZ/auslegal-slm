REASONS OF FULL TRIBUNAL OF LEGAL PROFESSION
TRIBUNAL FOR DECISION GIVEN ON
8 NOVEMBER 2005
(Section 409
Legal Practice Act)
The legal practitioner was admitted to practice as a barrister and
solicitor of the Supreme Court of Victoria on 1 April 1982.
practitioner was employed by T.A.C. Law Pty Ltd as Group Manager of the Legal
Costs Department up until 29 February 2004
and thereafter as the holder of a
corporate practising certificate with Transport Accident
Commission.
Between 31 January 2000 and 22 April 2004 the legal
practitioner stole 21 cheques totalling $416,254.67 from the Transport Accident
Commission and, for that purpose, falsified accounting records.
proceeds of 19 cheques totalling $366,745.67 were credited to the legal
practitioner’s personal bank account.
On 29 April 2005 the legal
practitioner, before Mr Justice Bongiorno in the Supreme Court of Victoria,
pleaded guilty to 14 counts
of theft and 9 counts of false accounting in respect
of the thefts and false accounting perpetrated by her in the course of her
by T.A.C. Law Pty Ltd and the Transport Accident
Commission.
On 3 June 2005 the legal practitioner was sentenced to be
imprisoned for four years with a minimum term of two years. The legal
practitioner
is currently serving that sentence.
Pursuant to the
provisions of section 151 of the
Legal Practice Act 1
Act”) and the obligation cast upon it by the provisions of that section,
the Law Institute of Victoria Limited
(LIV) has brought before the Full Tribunal
the charges against the legal practitioner as set out in the notice pursuant to
151 of the Act (annexure 1).
On the hearing before the Full
Tribunal Mr Paul A D Bean, Manager, Complaints and Dispute Resolutions, delegate
of LIV appeared on
behalf of LIV. There was no appearance by or on behalf of
the legal practitioner.
Correspondence between Professional Standards of
LIV, the legal practitioner’s husband and Mr Geoff Croxford of Voitin
Davis, solicitor for the legal practitioner, indicated the legal
practitioner did not contest the charges brought against her and
did not intend
to appear nor be represented before the Full Tribunal. It was stated the legal
practitioner consented to a cancellation
of her practising certificate and did
not intend to again seek a practising certificate. It was stated the legal
practitioner was
not in a position to pay LIV costs.
We were informed the
legal practitioner had not applied to LIV for renewal of her practising
certificate and currently did not hold
a practising certificate.
Tribunal is satisfied the legal practitioner is guilty of misconduct at common
law in the course of engaging in legal practice
in relation to the charges set
out in the notice.
The Full Tribunal is satisfied the legal practitioner
is guilty of a serious breach of trust and dishonesty. We are satisfied the
legal practitioner is not a fit and proper person to be a member of the legal
profession.
The certified certificate of conviction of the legal
practitioner in the Supreme Court of Victoria Criminal Division is annexed
“annexure 2”).
The Full Tribunal orders that:
For a period of ten years
from 8 November 2005 the legal practitioner may not apply for any practising
certificate.
The legal practitioner not at
any time be issued with a practising certificate authorising the holding of
trust money.
The legal practitioner be
referred to the Supreme Court with a recommendation that her name be struck off
the roll of practitioners.
The legal practitioner pay to
LIV its costs of and incidental to these proceedings fixed in the sum of
There be a stay of sixty days
for payment of costs.
