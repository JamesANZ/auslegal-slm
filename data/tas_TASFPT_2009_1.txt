A Ricketts v Tasmanian Plantation Pty Ltd and Forest Practices Authority [2009] TASFPT 1 (3 April 2009)
File No: 4/08 F D1/2009
A Ricketts Appellant
Tasmanian Plantation Pty Ltd and Forest Practices Authority Respondents
This was the hearing of an appeal against the decision of the Forest
Practices Authority to grant an application for declaration of
land at Davies
Road, Parkham as a Private Timber Reserve No. 1893.
The appeal was
heard at Hobart on 27
By arrangement with the
parties, there were no appearances.
Application, decision and appeal
Application
was made for the declaration of a Private Timber Reserve No. 1893 at
Davies Road, Parkham. Objection was made to the
application, by the
Appellant. Private Forests Tasmania made a recommendation to the Forest
Practices Authority to approve the application,
which the Authority
Appellant, being a ‘prescribed person’ under Section 7(4) of the
Forest Practices Act 1985
as owner of land adjoining the boundaries of
the proposed Private Timber Reserve, lodged the present appeal.
grounds of appeal were that the Appellant would suffer a direct and material
disadvantage, in that declaration of a Private Timber
Reserve would allow for
the potential expansion and future rotations of an existing plantation, without
control by the provisions
of the Planning Scheme which would apply in the
absence of a Private Timber Reserve.
Appellant referred specifically to danger from fire; adverse effects upon the
native wildlife the presence of which he enjoyed;
an extension of wind throw
effects detrimental to the natural forest on his land; and adverse effects upon
his water source.
other parties to the appeal did not contest that the appeal should be allowed.

order of the Tribunal is that pursuant to Section 9(6) of the
Practices Act 1985
, the Tribunal quashes the decision of the Forest

Private Timber Reserve application No. PTR 1893.
