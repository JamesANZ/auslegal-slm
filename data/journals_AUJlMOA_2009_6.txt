Conferences
22-23 April
Offshore Support Vessels – Asia Pacific,
Twenty three speakers – including senior managers from Bourbon
Offshore, Neste Shipping Oy, DNV, Rolls Royce Marine, Standard
Chartered Bank,
Inchcape Shipping Services, Swire Pacific Offshore, Farstad Shipping and Damen
Shipyards – will discuss commercial
trends in the OSV sector and their
implications on design, operations and logistics.
See further:
www.ibc-asia.com/osv-asiapacific
World Ocean Conference, Manado, Indonesia
This conference will be attended by Ministers and high level officials from
national governments and multilateral organisations to
discuss current issues.
Specific issues of discussion will include current marine developments relating
to climate change, and how
the world can use ocean resources.
See further:
www.woc2009.org/woc_home.php
Summit Down Under, Gold Coast
The National Marine Safety Committee’s summit on recreational boat
standards will provide an insight into how the standards
used in the world
market are changing to meet today’s challenges.
See further:
www.nmsc.gov.au
‘Natship 2009’ -
National Shipping
Industry Conference, Sydney
Convened by the Australian Maritime Safety Authority in association with the
Australian Shipowners Association, Ports Australia, and
Shipping Australia
National and international speakers will address issues including commercial
developments, new technologies, shop and port operations,
infrastructure
advances, ship safety and environment regulation and shipping’s future
See further:
www.amsa.gov.au/natship09
‘Marine Connectivity’ – Annual
Conference of Marine Sciences Association, Adelaide
Keynote speakers plus sessions, symposia and workshops will bring together
marine scientists from all disciplines to discuss and debate
the state of the
art for investigating marine connectivity, novel approaches and technical
advances, and the meaning of connectivity
in the oceans and coastal seas.
See further:
www.amsaconference.com.au
Annual Queensland Transport Infrastructure Summit,
Government departments, finance experts, engineering companies and transport
leaders will assess major infrastructure developments
and project priorities,
address Queensland’s economic and ecological challenges and discuss the
boom state’s transport
infrastructure future.
See further:
www.informa.com.au/qldtransport

‘The Commonwealth Navies: 100 years of

Registration will be at no cost but the number of delegates will be limited
by the size of the venue.
