RIGNEY, Amber Rose and MITCHELL, Korey Lee - Inquest Number 7/2021 (0985/2016), Responses [2022] SACorCResp 3 (21 April 2022)
Rigney, Amber Rose and Mitchell, Korey Lee (Coroner Schapel)
Recommendation 1
that the Premier of South
Australia, the Minister for Child Protection and the Chief Executive of Child
Protection cause a review
of all coronial and other recommendations relating to
child protection in the State of South Australia with a view to the
implementation
of the same.
Recommendation 2
that a complete review be
conducted in relation to all of the statutory obligations contained within the
Children and Young People (Safety) Act 2017
so as to ensure that practices
within the Department align with those statutory obligations. Such a review
should consist of an
examination of all documented and undocumented internal
Departmental procedures so as to ensure that they comply with all statutory
obligations contained within child protection legislation.
Response of the South Australian Government to Recommendations 1 and
On 25 May 2022,
Ms Kate Alexander was appointed by the Minister for Child Protection to conduct
a review pursuant to recommendations
1 and 2. Ms Alexander is an Executive
Director of the Office of the Senior Practitioner for the New South Wales
Department of Family
and Community Services and has extensive knowledge of child
protection and vast experience as an expert witness in NSW coronial proceedings
and Royal Commission hearings. Ms Alexander was appointed to conduct a review
All coronial and
other recommendations relating to child protection in the State of South
Australia since 2010 and the implementation
of the same.
All statutory
obligations contained within the
Children and Young People (Safety) Act 2017
the extent to which practices in the Department for Child Protection, and other
State Government agencies as may be appropriate,
align with those statutory
obligations.
With respect to
recommendation 1, the terms of reference of Ms Alexander’s review also
required consideration of the most appropriate
lead agency(s) for the ongoing
implementation of coronial and other recommendations. Ms Alexander’ report
has been completed.
Recommendation 3
that on completion of such review, an assessment be made to ensure that the
carrying out of all statutory obligations under the
Children and Young People
(Safety) Act 2017
are fully resourced to enable those duties and obligations to
be carried out without exception.
Response of the South Australian Government
The Government
is committed to ensuring adequate resourcing is available to ensure all
statutory obligations under the
Children and Young People (Safety) Act 2017
be met and will undertake the assessment as recommended. Ms Alexander’s
review and report supported that assessment. Pursuant
section 169
Children and Young People (Safety) Act 2017
, a review of the operation of the
Act commenced in September 2022, and is being informed by an ongoing community
consultation process.
This provides an opportunity to support the review of the
statutory obligations identified in recommendation
Recommendation 4
that the Chief
Executive of Child Protection remind all staff of the Department of the need,
especially where there are allegations
involving abuse of an illicit drug
placing a child at risk, to consider whether in the circumstances police powers
in their capacity
as child protection officers pursuant to the provisions of the
Children and Young People (Safety) Act 2017
should be utilised to assist in any
assessment or investigation pursuant to that Act.
Response of the South Australian Government
On 14 July 2022,
the Chief Executive of the Department wrote to all Department for Child
Protection staff emphasising the need, when
planning or conducting a child
protection investigation or assessment, for staff to consider whether to request
that South Australia
Police exercise child protection powers in their capacity
as authorised child protection officers under the
Children and Young People
(Safety) Act 2017
, particularly where there are allegations that the use of an
illicit drug by a parent or care giver is placing a child or young person
risk. The Department’s implementation of its Alcohol and Other Drugs
Practice Excellence Strategy ensures that practice
guidance in this area is
regularly reviewed and updated so that it remains
contemporary.
