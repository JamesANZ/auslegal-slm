LEONG YI HUI, Adelene - Inquest Number 94/2020 (1609/2014), Responses [2022] SACorCResp 8 (7 June 2022)
Leong Yi Hui, Adelene (Coroner White)
Recommendation 1
recommended
the Minister receive formal advice from the AIHS and SafeWork:
the urgent need for reform of the regulation and governance of amusement rides
and devices in Australia;
urge the creation and implementation in all Australian jurisdictions
nationwide regulatory process to verify and record design registrations for
amusement devices and rides to ensure that every amusement
device and ride in
Australia is subject to the same process of design registration;
common application procedure for plant registration to ensure that every plant
registration has met the same criteria, regardless
of where it is registered in
or regulation that plant registration will not be granted unless a design
registration certificate has been endorsed and verified
by the State or
Territory regulator;
national database of design registration numbers and plant registration numbers
that is accessible by regulators of all States and
Territories;
nationwide panel of qualified persons be established to assess, annually, the
skills of competent persons inspecting amusement rides
and devices to verify
which aspects of expertise the competent person possesses;
annual assessment of the competent persons including a requirement of mandatory
participation in a continuing education scheme;
mandatory national electronic logbook system for all amusement devices and rides
in Australia that can be accessed by all authorised
people necessary for
inspections and audits in any jurisdiction;
to ensure the logbook for an amusement ride or device must include a design
registration certificate, a plant registration certificate
and a statement from
the manufacturer of the device or ride confirming their criteria for eligibility
of patrons to use the ride
to enforce that a notice issued by a regulator in any jurisdiction concerning an
amusement device or ride requiring improvements
to safety, reports of injuries
to patrons as well as any other critical safety issues be available for
inspection in the logbook
for that ride or device as well as on a national
Responses from the Minister for Industrial Relations and Public Sector
The Government
agrees that significant regulatory reform is required to the governance of
amusement rides and devices in Australia.
Miss Leong’s death is a tragic
event that has shown that a consistent national approach to regulation is
required to enable
a direct and strong impact on the safety on amusement
To achieve this
SafeWork SA as a member of Safe Work Australia (SWA) and the Head of Workplace
Safety Authorities (HWSA), has progressed
the outcomes and recommendations of
inquest to both SWA and HWSA.
national policy body representing the interests of the Commonwealth, states and
territories, as well as workers and employers
to drive, develop, evaluate and
revise national work health and safety and workers’ compensation policies
and supporting strategies.
HWSA is made up of representatives from work health
and safety regulators across Australia and New Zealand to work together to
and implement best practice in work health and safety in the areas of
operational policy and legislative matters, education and enforcement.
SafeWork SA
tabled the recommendation at the SWA meeting of 30 June 2022, and sought to
commence the discussion about a nationally
consistent approach to amusement
device design registration and the required legislative reform. It was agreed
that HWSA would lead
this work operationally and regularly report back to the
Safe Work Australia meetings.
HWSA considered
this matter at their meeting on 3 August 2022 and agreed to establish an
Engineering Special Advisory Group (ESAG)
to address the recommendations in
their scope of work.
The work of the
ESAG is ongoing and SafeWork SA remains an actively engaged member to ensure the
recommendations are considered closely
by all regulators.
Work Health and Safety Regulations
Amendments to
Work Health and Safety Regulations 2012
were made on 25 August 2023 to
commence on 25 December 2023.
regulations adopted from model work health and safety regulations require more
detail to be included in amusement device log
books including complete histories
of notices issued by work health and safety inspectors, full operational dates,
maintenance history,
faults, storage details, the operators’ history, and
identification.
commencement date amusement device owners must comply with the new regulations
and must ensure the log book for an amusement
device record:
occasion on which the device is stored, details of the storage;
details of the
maintenance of the device
for each day on
which the device is operated, the number of hours for which it is operated
the total number
of hours for which the device has ever been operated
details of any
faults, or other matters relevant to the safety of the device, identified during
its operation
specific details
for each person who operates the device and whether the person has been provided
with instruction and training in
the proper operation of the device
occasion on which instruction or training in the proper operation of the device
is provided to the person with the date,
a summary of the instruction or
training; and the name and qualifications of the instructor or trainer
the details of
each statutory notice issued in relation to the device, including the date the
notice was issued, the reasons for issuing
the notice; and any action taken in
response to the notice
if a notice was
issued under corresponding work health and safety law the location of the device
when the notice was issued.
Operators must
also be able to produce any information within the logbook upon request and
failure to do so is an offence.
The aim of the
new regulations is to ensure a full comprehensive record of the amusement
devices is available to regulators as devices
travel across Australia.
