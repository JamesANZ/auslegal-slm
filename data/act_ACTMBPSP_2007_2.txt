Dr PB [2007] ACTMBPSP 2 (1 January 2007)
Medical Board of the Australian Capital Territory
Professional Standards Panel Outcomes
The subject matter of the Inquiry consisted of the following allegations:
In relation to his patient Dr PB:
to act in a timely manner to arrange follow up investigation and/or
appropriate treatment for a patient with a potentially
health or life
threatening condition;
to adequately communicate to the patient the possible diagnosis of
ectopic pregnancy when no foetal tissue was found following
termination procedure;
failed to adequately
communicate the need to seek urgent medical review in case of
deterioration of the patient�s condition.
Dr PB stated that it was difficult to get good images with the
older ultrasound equipment owned by his employer. He further
that it would be much better if patients attended with ultrasound
images taken by a radiology provider, but for reasons of privacy
cost this was not always practicable.
Dr PB stated his regret at the distress caused to his
patient. He also stated that as a direct result of this Inquiry,
he had instigated
with his employer protocols to cover such situations
in the future.
He further stated that since the incident there had been staff
changes at the clinic, and that a new ultrasound machine had been
obtained. He stated that the patient information sheets had been
updated, and other measures instituted to prevent further incidents
The Panel found that Dr PB had not contravened the required
standards of practice, nor had he put the safety of the public at
The Panel�s reasons for its decision were that it:
felt that Dr PB was not
neglectful or negligent, and dealt with an unexpected operative finding
in accordance with the protocol
laid down by his employer as
expeditiously as was possible in the circumstances;
was pleased that his recommendation to obtain a new ultrasound machine was successful;
was pleased with the new
forms and proforma discharge notification, which were suggested by Dr PB
and which will improve post operative
care had been adopted by his
employer; and
was impressed by the attitude of Dr P.B in its presence in expressing his regrets.
