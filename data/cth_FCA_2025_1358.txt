persons adopted by the biological descendants in accordance with Ngadju

Adoption, under Ngadju tradition and custom,
refers to the situation where a child is 'grown up' by a relative or someone
a biological relationship, either because they have been 'gifted' to
them, or left in their care, as the biological parents are not
in a position to
care for them. This applies regardless of whether or not the child has been
formally adopted under the non-Aboriginal
legal system.
