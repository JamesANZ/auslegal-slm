Tasmanian Planning Scheme - King Island
Planning authority
King Island
Date of decision
30 June 2025
The draft amendment is approved under
section 40Q
Land Use Planning and Approvals Act 1993
John Ramsay
Executive Commissioner
REASONS FOR DECISION
On 18 October 2024, the
Commission decided to modify the draft King Island Local Provisions Schedule
(draft LPS). The King Island
LPS (as modified) became effective on 18 December
As part of its 18 October 2024 decision, the Commission found that the draft
LPS required substantial modification and accordingly,
section 35KB
Act, the Commission directed the planning authority to prepare a draft amendment
to the King Island LPS and to submit the
draft amendment to the Commission after
the LPS came into effect.
The subject draft amendment is one of five draft amendments to the King
Island LPS prepared in response to the Commission’s
direction. The King
Island planning authority has publicly exhibited the draft amendment in
accordance with
section 40G
and provided the Commission with its
section 40K
The draft amendment is to apply the Landscape
Conservation Zone and remove the KIC-S1.0 Fences in the King Island Rural Area
Area Plan (SAP) and the Local Area Objectives overlay for clause
KIC-P4.2.1 from the following properties:
Road, Yambacoona folio of the Register 208063/1
Road, Sea Elephant folios of the Register 163546/2 and 163546/3
road reserves to the road centreline.
Site information
The land at Dickers Road is predominately vegetated, the TasVeg4.0 layer on
theLIST indicates the land contains areas of two threatened
communities; (NME)
Melaleuca ericifolia
swamp forest and (WGK)
Eucalyptus globulus
King Island forest. The land is accessible from a
track however does not have a frontage to a public road. The land is bounded to
the north, east and south by land in the Environmental Management Zone, the land
to the southwest is in the Particular Purpose Zone.
The land at Reekara Road (163546/2) is predominately vegetated, similarly
TasVeg4.0 indicates the land contains patches of (WBR)
brookeriana
wet forest also a threatened community. The adjoining land on
Reekara Road (163546/3) is a rectangular lot with an access strip along
north boundary of the adjoining lot. The property is heavily vegetated but is
not identified as containing any threatened communities.
The land is currently in the King Island Particular Purpose Zone (PPZ) - King
Island Rural Area and subject to the Local Area Objectives
overlay KIC-P4.2.1.
Additionally, the land is contained within the KIC-S1.0 Fences in the King
Island Rural Area SAP as land within
the King Island PPZ is considered a Rural
Land to the north of the adjoining parcels is in the Landscape Conservation
and Environmental Management Zones, to the east Environmental
Management and to
the south and west the Particular Purpose Zone.
Issues raised in representations
The draft amendment was referred to TasWater under
section 56S
Water and Sewerage Industry Act 2008
. In response
TasWater provided a statement that it had reviewed the draft amendment and had
no representation to make.
No other representations were received in relation to the draft
Planning authority’s response to the
representations
The planning authority did not recommend any
modification to the draft amendment.
Consideration of the draft amendment
section 40M
Land Use Planning and Approvals Act 1993
the Commission is required to consider the draft amendment to the LPS and the
representations, statements and recommendations
contained in the planning
authority’s section 40K report and any information obtained at a
opposing representations were received, and the Commission dispensed with a
hearing in accordance with
section 40(2A)
of the Act.

, that applies in relation to the land to which the

its approval of the draft LPS the Commission determined that it met the LPS
criteria. As part of that decision, it follows that
the Commission also

further submissions were received raising any matters that warrant further

Commission Consideration
Commission’s decision on the King Island LPS established that it accepted
the evidence in support of the application of
the Landscape Conservation Zone to
the subject properties and it considers application of the zone is consistent
with Guideline No.1.
Commission notes the removal of the King Island Particular Purpose Zone - King
Island Rural Area and the associated Local Area
Objectives overlay removes the
requirement for the land to be included in the KIC-S1.0 Fences in the King
Island Rural Area SAP.
its decision on the King Island LPS, the Commission considered that the
modification was a substantial modification as there may
be a public interest.
The Commission considers that exhibition of the draft amendment has given
sufficient opportunity for any matters
of public interest to be raised. As
representations were not received in relation to the draft amendment, the
Commission remains
satisfied that the draft amendment is consistent with the LPS
criteria at
section 34(2)
of the Act.
Decision on draft amendment
Commission is satisfied that the draft amendment meets the LPS criteria and
gives its approval.
