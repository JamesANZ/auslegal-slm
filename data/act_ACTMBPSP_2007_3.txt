Dr PC [2007] ACTMBPSP 3 (1 January 2007)
Medical Board of the Australian Capital Territory
Professional Standards Panel Outcomes
The subject matter of the inquiry consists of allegations that Dr PC:
failed to conduct an appropriate examination of his patient;
failed to take an adequate history from his patient;
failed to record adequate clinical notes of your examination of his patient; and
Dr PC is not generally competent to practice as a medical practitioner.
The panel considered the computer record of consultation of Dr PC
with his patient 2004, together with the Patient Summary generated
the computer software. Dr PC stated that the record was a true
record of the consultation. He further stated that he had no
recollection whatsoever of the consultation.
On questioning, Dr PC agreed that the record might be referred to
as �inadequate� and explained his usual practice with a new
He agreed that no details of examination had been entered
into the record and that no details of allergies had been entered into
the record. He also agreed that no detail of past or current
medical history had been entered into the record.
He agreed that no temperature had been taken or recorded.
He stated that he would have enquired of his patient as to his allergies
but would probably have made no enquiry as to past history or other
current illnesses or treatments for a clinical presentation
such as the
patient presented with. Dr PC stated both in his written evidence
and orally that he felt that he could not be blamed
for the death of his
patient which occurred some 40 days after his consultation. The
panel agreed with this statement.
The panel found that allegations 1, 2 and 3 were proved and that
Dr PC had contravened the required standards of practice. However
the panel determined that allegation 4 was not proven and that Dr PC was
generally competent to practice medicine.
Dr PC was reprimanded for failing to maintain adequate clinical
records and for failing to take or prove that he took an adequate
history and conduct an appropriate examination.
