Under the RTA, tenants have
the right to keep a pet at their rental premises with the landlord’s
The tenant is responsible for any damage or nuisance caused by
a pet they keep at the premises.
Landlords have the right to apply to
Consumer Protection for an order allowing them to refuse the pet request.
When an application is received, the Commissioner is required to either:
approve the
landlord’s application; or
landlord to approve the tenant’s request.
Commissioner may approve this application if satisfied that the grounds claimed
by the landlord are established. In this case,
the claimed ground is:
The premises are
unsuitable for keeping the pet.
The landlord is responsible for
demonstrating that the above reason applies.

The RTA provides examples of why a premises may be unsuitable, such
as a lack of fencing and open spaces.

as the toileting of the requested pet, commenting that the back patio is paved,
and the front yard only has artificial lawn with no fencing.
