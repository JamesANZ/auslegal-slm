Dr PG [2007] ACTMBPSP 7 (1 January 2007)
Medical Board of the Australian Capital Territory
Professional Standards Panel Outcomes
The subject matter of the Inquiry consisted of allegations that Dr PG failed to:
take an adequate patient history;
an adequate examination and thus failed to diagnose a fractured rib and
collapsed lung;
consider the possibility of any other diagnosis or conduct an
investigation or order any other diagnostic examination to exclude
diagnoses or confirm your diagnosis;
provide a response to a letter of complaint from the patient.
An agreed Statement of Facts was presented and Dr PG interviewed
by the panel. Dr PG stated that she has a reasonable recollection
of the visit made by her to the patient. She has a good memory of
the length of the consultation and stated that she would have
present with the patient for about fifteen minutes. She described
her history taking and focussed examination of the painful
She stated that the patient talked very freely to her, and there was no
obvious sign of shortness of breath or difficulty
in breathing.
She denied being told of any history of trauma, despite a direct
question. She stated that she did not use a stethoscope
occasion as she believed that with no trauma the condition was
muscular. She did not take the blood pressure or perform
examination apart from the local area. She had recorded notes at the
time. The panel agreed that the notes clearly mentioned
�No Trauma�.
The panel found that Dr PG did not:
fail to take an adequate history and conduct an appropriate examination.
consider alternative
diagnoses, but advised speedy follow up if the symptoms failed to
fail to provide a response to the letter of complaint.
