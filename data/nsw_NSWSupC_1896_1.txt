Rose v. Mitchell
Supreme Court of New South Wales
Cohen J., 24 March 1896
Sydney Morning Herald
, 25 March 1896, p. 4
No. 3 Jury Court. - (Before his Honor Mr. Justice Cohen.)
ROSE V. MITCHELL Mr. Macintyre (instructed by Mr. H. Levien) appeared for the plaintiff, Thomas Rose, who, on behalf of Mary Rose,
his daughter, sued John Mitchell for seduction, damages being laid at �500. There was no appearance for the defendant. The case for
the plaintiff was that Mitchell was a friend of the family, and had been acquainted with the daughter for some years. Mary Rose stated
the defendant had promised to marry her, and the result of his intimacy was that she had become a mother. The jury returned a verdict
for the plaintiff for �110.
