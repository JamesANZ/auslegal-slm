HCCC v WHYTE (No 2) [2012] NSWPST 4 (16 February 2012)
HEALTH PRACTITIONER REGULATION NATIONAL
LAW (NSW) No 86a
PSYCHOLOGY TRIBUNAL OF NSW
INQUIRY UNDER
SECTION 167
STEPHEN JAMES WHYTE
MATTER NUMBER: PS 02/2011
REGISTRATION NUMBER: PSY
HCCC v WHYTE (No 2)
[2012] NSWPST 4
DATE OF INTERIM ORDERS:
15 February 2012
Having made a finding of professional misconduct (see HCCC v Whyte (No 1)
[2012] NSWPST 2
the Tribunal Orders in accordance with s 167E of the National
Law the following conditions be imposed upon the Registration of Stephen
Whyte until further Order of the Tribunal:
Respondent is to provide within 14 days of the date hereof written confirmation
from the principal of each of the practices to
which he currently provides
contracted services (the Tribunal notes it has been advised there are five such
practices as at the date
hereof) to the Psychology Council of New South Wales
(Council) and to the Psychology Tribunal of NSW (Tribunal) that he has provided
a full copy of the Reasons for Decision document (HCCC v WHYTE (No 1)
and that they (i.e. the principals of the practices) have read this
document in its entirety. The written confirmation is to provide
the name of the
principal in the practice and contact details for that person.
Respondent is not to work in any other practice other than those nominated by
his legal representative in the letter to the Tribunal
dated 9 February 2012
without the written permission of the Council. If permission is granted by the
Council, it may do so subject
to any conditions it sees fit to
Respondent is to engage the services of a professional supervisor within 21 days
of the date hereof. The Tribunal approves Dr
Chris Lennings (see paragraph 2.1
of the letter to the Tribunal dated 9 February 2012 from the Respondent’s
legal representative)
as an Approved Supervisor. Should Dr Lennings be unable or
unwilling to act in this role, the Council may approve another person
Approved Supervisor on the written application of the Respondent. Such
supervisor is to be of an equivalent standing in the
profession to Dr Lennings
and is not to be a practitioner with whom the Respondent has previously worked
and/or has any connection
with the Complaint before this Tribunal. The
professional supervision is to be for a minimum period of two hours face to face
supervision
per week and at the direction of the Approved Supervisor. The
Respondent is to provide the Council with a copy of the written authorisation
and direction he provides to the Approved Supervisor to provide reports to the
Council and Tribunal every six weeks from the date
of engagement and at any
other time should issues of concern arise.
Respondent is to provide a copy of the Mental Health Care Plan prepared by his
General Practitioner (the Tribunal refers to paragraph
4.1 of the letter to the
Tribunal dated 9 February 2012 from the Respondent’s legal representative)
to the Council and Tribunal
within seven days of the date hereof. The Respondent
is also to provide the name and contact details of the practitioner providing
him with services in accordance with the Mental Health Care Plan. The Respondent
is to authorise and direct the practitioner providing
him with services in
accordance with the Mental Health Care Plan to provide a copy of a report at the
end of every six sessions attended
by the Respondent to the Tribunal and
Council. The Respondent is to provide a copy of this authorisation and direction
to the Council
and Tribunal.
Respondent is to stay under the care of his current General Practitioner. The
Respondent is to provide a copy of the written authorisation
and direction to
his treating General Practitioner to notify the Council and Tribunal if the
General Practitioner becomes aware of
any issues that may affect the
Respondent’s ability to practice as a registered psychologist. The
Respondent is to provide
a full copy of the Reasons for Decision document (HCCC
v WHYTE (No 1)
[2012] NSWPST 2
to his General Practitioner.
Respondent is only to consult clients during standard business hours and when
there is at least one other health practitioner
present in the
Respondent is to limit all contact with clients outside of consultations to
matters concerning the making of appointments and
to referral the client for
other treatment.
Respondent is to advise all clients that he has been the subject of a finding of
professional misconduct and that he has conditions
on his Registration pending
the final determination of the Tribunal. In this regard the Respondent is to
make a copy of the Reasons
for Decision document (HCCC v WHYTE (No 1)
available to his clients in addition to a copy of these Orders. A
written acknowledgment from each client that they have been so
advised and had
the documents made available to them is to be placed on the client’s file
held by the Respondent.
Publication
A copy of these Orders are to be forwarded by the Registry to:
1) The Respondent and his legal advisor,
2) The HCCC,
3) The Psychology Council of New South Wales,
4) The Psychology Board of Australia,
5) as the Council and Board see fit.
Dated: 16 February 2012
